What it's all about

Medications are being prescribed at an alarming rate around the world. According to the FDA, over 4.5 billion prescriptions were filled, and Adverse Drug Reactions (ADRs) caused over 106,000 deaths and hospitalized over 2M people in 2017 in the US alone. In developing countries, where prescription drugs are available over the counter, the death rates due to ADRs are even higher. In India, for example, 52% of the population admits to self-medicating prescription drugs in order to save doctor consultancy costs. That’s nearly 690M people in India who are at risk of ADR.

While there is a severe shortage of doctors around the world, there has been, however, a great proliferation of smartphone users. There are nearly 2.4B smartphone users with Internet access around the world and the number is expected to surpass 5B by 2020. This has led to a surge in online options to get the needed health care. Although these methods are cheaper and more easily accessible, the providers often do not have the full information on patients and treatment may lead to more potential risks. The Ok to Take app is going to turn these things around and protect patients from ADRs.

How it will help me

Available 24/7 at your fingertips, using innovative drug recognition technology, an extensive database and a user-friendly interface, the OK to Take app allows you keep an up-to-date list of your current medications and allergies and answer basic drug interaction questions, e.g.: Is this new drug safe to take with my current medications? Will I have an allergic reaction to this medication? The app can warn you about a potentially harmful drug interaction, and notify you when it’s important to contact your doctor, thus protecting your health.

Add drugs to check interaction

The OK to Take app is extremely easy to use. A list of medications is created by adding them to the app either by taking a picture of the blister pack or by typing in the drug’s name. A quick check to see if any on the current or newly added medications can cause any harm be done by touch of a button.

See your risks

The OK to Take app checks for potentially dangerous interactions among a group of medications. Interactions will be classified into three risk types, high, medium and low. It is recommended that you stop taking a medication that leads to a high risk interaction and consult the prescribing health care provider. For medium and low risks, it is likely safe to take the medications but it’s best to read the prescribing information that accompanies the drug and/or discuss with your healthcare provider. Take your health seriously.

Create & Share Reports

The app creates medication reports that you can share with others. Reports that list current and/or past medications, along with drug allergies, can provide a clearer picture of your health to a doctor, who can then make safe prescribing recommendations. This app will generate the report that can be downloaded to your smartphone and/or sent directly to a health care provider or family member.
